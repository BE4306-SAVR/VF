Virtual Studi​​o Set Project

Chantelle Lam Pei Xuan

clam69.21@ichat.sp.edu.sg

click on the image to experience the Interactive VR
Password: yJUWPVBVLC

CONCEPT STATEMENT:
My concept is derived from my love of 70s Retro Interior Design. This style of interior is well-loved by many for several reasons, such as the prevalence of wooden materials and organic shapes, as well as an abundance of greenery and its tendency to not shy away from colours, which is a stark contrast to popular cotemporary interiors of today that are minimal in detail and often devoid of colour.
The idea I imagined for this virtual set was a TV show by a retro furniture company to showcase their catalogues. While showcasing the furniture, the host and guests would sit around the revolving showcase table, enjoy drinks, and have good conversation.
An iconic retro design that I want to incorporate is the sunken lounge. I think it gives a very welcoming vibe to a room, which is what I’m going for. I want this set design to feel warm and friendly to both guests and those watching it on broadcast.
Warm lighting will be in abundance throughout the set, brought about by the funky circular lighting on the ceiling. Several podiums will flank the lounge area where furniture from previous broadcasts will be featured and shown off.
To truly honor the 70s aesthetic, my design would overall aim to have its fair share of quirky features, such as the presence of bold, wacky patterns and the random egg-like planters. Lastly, I incorporated a performance stage for singers and musicians to accompany the broadcast with songs of the past.

Bus Shelter Project

Chantelle Lam Pei Xuan

clam69.21@ichat.sp.edu.sg

Bus Shelter Project

click on the image to experience the Panoramic VR

CONCEPTUAL STATEMENT:
To me, bus stops have and always will be an integral place in people's daily routines. Thus, I want my bus stop to bring out the sense of community, or 'kampung spirit' that comes with being in a bus stop. I also aim to bring more attention to traditional architecture of this region, as I feel that traditional styles and aesthetics are highly underrated despite having many pleasing qualities that are often absent in modern architecture, such as the use of ornamentation and natural materials. Since bus stops have remained so simple for so many years, I would like to propose a design that utilises traditional elements in order to further the significance of today’s bus stops.

A key feature is the layout of the seats, made up of large, stacked benches. By stacking the seats, more available seating can be provided without their view of incoming buses being blocked by those sitting in front. Additionally, I designed my benches wholly, without segmentation like you see in usual bus stops. I want to promote a fun and inviting atmosphere, where users aren’t immediately compartmented from others and feel free to spread themselves out more. In addition, I opted to designate a priority wheelchair area towards the middle, close to the bus entrance, so that those with mobility issues can have more ease when boarding.

The structure itself, which was inspired by Malay Architecture, provides high ceilings that elevate the feeling of a shared space amongst its users, as well as a traditional column and beam layout that gives users an opportunity to admire in their surroundings.
The gabled roof is decorated with ornamental carvings that serve as a semi-porous opening for hot air to escape upwards, promoting cooler air in the bus stop throughout the day. This natural method of cooling was also adapted from Malay Architecture, which has been suited to combat the hot and humid weather of this region.
A key feature is the use of green roofs which provide thermal resistance from the sun throughout the day, promoting a cooler environment in the bus stop. The use of vegetation can also greatly enhance the experience of being in the bus stop as they are pleasant and relaxing to look at, hence it was important to incorporate them into the immediate landscape surrounding the bus stop.

I find most bus stops to be very dimly lit at night, such that they feel unsafe to be in or hard to make out on the street. Hence, I incorporated pockets of light leading to and as part of the green roof, in order to illuminate its presence to pedestrians. For interior lighting, I kept it to a minimal number of brightly lit sources, which I feel illuminate the wooden environment evenly which has a pleasant effect, as opposed to using multiple line lights or spot lights.

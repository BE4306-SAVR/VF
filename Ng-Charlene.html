Virtual Studi​​o Set Project

Ng Charlene

charlene.21@ichat.sp.edu.sg

click on the image to experience the Interactive VR
Password: meJGcVYzXT

CONCEPT STATEMENT:
Despite their apparent differences, neo-futurism and news have a similar futuristic perspective. Neo-futurism is all about cutting-edge, one-of-a-kind designs, while the news informs us of said cutting-edge designs and technologies. At time, predictions of the future are discussed in the news as well. With this in mind, the design takes advantage of this unique connection to create a space that has the original zones of a news studio while adopting organic forms that reflect the studio's futurist viewpoint. Furthermore, the modern approach is in line with the space's primary function, it being a virtual studio with chroma key importation.
The studio is divided into four sections: Anchor Desk, Weather Dome, Analysts Desk, and the Guests Here. A news broadcast always starts with a view of the Anchor Desk. One can discuss breaking news, major stories of the day, or regular local news here. The Weather Dome is the next stop. A weatherperson would enter the dome, where big green screens (including a fake airwell) would show clear views of the weather for that day/week. Following that, economists take their seats at the analyst desk to provide an assessment of the economic state, helped by pictorials and graphs displayed on green screens. Finally, invited guests have their own cozy interview place. This zone also has a ramp to account for level differences such that guests who are in wheelchairs can get there comfortably. In addition to the mentioned, green screens are placed in the intermediate spaces such that they can be made useful too.
For the material palette, glass, steel & polished concrete i used to stay true to the neo-futurist style. In addition to this, pastel carpets, stones and lighting help to offset the coldness that the previous materials evoke by imparting a sense of warmth in the space.
Overall, the studio hopes to present an innovative news telecast concept that is representative of mankind's future potential.

Bus Shelter Project

Ng Charlene

charlene.21@ichat.sp.edu.sg

Bus Shelter Project

click on the image to experience the Panoramic VR

CONCEPTUAL STATEMENT:
The idea of warmth is the main design concept in my bus stop. In order to create an inviting and comfortable pit stop, the creation of warmth was especially important to me.
This naturally made me lean towards curved forms which are softer in nature, creating that sense of coziness. Following that, the same ideology was brought into the other aspects of the bus stop. One example of this is the materiality, which consists of wood and glass. Wood's natural brown colour establishes the warmth while the frosted green glass panels allow a comfortable amount of daylight to enter and illuminate the space.
Not only does the glass control the amount of light entering the space, it is also essential in terms of visibility by allowing users to have a clear view of the incoming bus.
More considerations in the design include integration with other modes of transport (cycling), convenient waste disposal integration, information boards, accessibility (no level drops, PWD waiting point) & thermal comfort (wind - ceiling fans, rain - extended roof to cover the gap between the roof and the bus).
Overall, the design strives to make the daily occurrence of waiting for public transportation more enjoyable thermally & in a safe and comfortable environment.
